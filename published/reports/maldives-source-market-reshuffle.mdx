## Executive summary

No single number captures how volatile the demand side of Maldives tourism has become better than this one: in 2019, **China** sent the Maldives nearly **300,000** visitors and was comfortably its largest market. In 2021, it sent **2,000** — a 99% collapse, as China's zero-COVID policy shut down outbound travel entirely. By 2025, China was back at **329,000** and #1 again. In between, the country's top-market table was rewritten twice over.

<Chart artifact="arrivals-by-market" />

The order of the leaderboard tells the story on its own:

- **2019** — China, India, Italy, Germany, UK
- **2023** — India, Russia, China
- **2025** — China, Russia, UK, Germany, Italy

Three different #1 markets in six years. The resort base didn't move; the world that fills it did.

## The three movers

**China — the V.** A near-total collapse in 2020–22 under zero-COVID, then a steep recovery once outbound travel reopened. China's return is the single biggest swing in the recent data and the reason the 2025 table looks like 2019 again at the top.

**India — surge, then a sharp reversal.** With China shut, India became the Maldives' **#1 market in 2021** (292,000). Then it fell hard — from 209,000 in 2023 to about 131,000 in 2024, a ~37% drop widely reported as the fallout of a 2024 India–Maldives diplomatic row and a consumer boycott. India has not recovered to its peak.

**Russia — the war-era riser.** From 83,000 in 2019 to 279,000 in 2025, Russia climbed to the #2 slot as the Maldives stayed open to Russian travellers through a period when many Western destinations did not.

Through all of it, the **European base** — the UK, Germany and Italy — grew steadily and quietly, the ballast under a churning top of the table.

## The honest limit

This is a **descriptive** record, not a model. The movements are real and precisely measured, but the *causes* — China's outbound ban, India's boycott, Russia's reorientation — are drawn from public reporting and the timing fit, not from a structural estimate. That estimate is the next layer: tying each market's arrivals to its own income, prices and exchange rate to ask not just *what* moved but *how sensitive* each market is. This report is the foundation that layer is built on.

## Methodology

Annual tourist arrivals by country of nationality, from the Maldives Monetary Authority's by-market series (China 153, Russia 116, India 169, UK 127, Germany 142, Italy 132), 2010–2025. Counts are arrivals, not bednights, and reflect nationality as recorded at entry.
